Hispaniola

Carribean Paradise

Hispaniola is the 22nd largest island in the world,located in the Carribean Island group,the Greater Antilles. It is the second largest island in Carribean after Cuba and the tenth most populous island in the world.

Hispaniola is the site of the first permanent European settlement in the Americas, founded by Christopher Columbus on his voyage in 1492 and 1493.

Geography

Hispaniola is the second largest island of West Indies, with an area of 76,129 km. The name given to the island was Columbus. Hispaniola means "small Spain". The Tainos called the Island 'Quisquiya'. Mother Earth

From a geological point of view, the island of Hispaniola is part of two branches of the North America cordillera system and is about 660km long, 250km wide and extends west-easterly. In the west, the island of Hispaniola is separated by the approximately 90 km wide Windward Passage from Cuba and in the east by the Mona Passage of Puerto Rico which is up to 110km wide.

On the island of Hispaniola lie five large mountain chains.The highest mountains of the Carribean are in the Dominican Republic. The highest mountain is the Pico Durate with 3098 meters above sea level. Also the deepest point of Carribean lies on Hispaniola the salt lake Enriquilio in the Southwest of Dominican Republic.

Hispaniola offers four vegetation zones and four climatic zones.More than 5000 plant speciec many ofthem are endemic, make the island a paradise for nature lovers.The animal world is also convincing, it is although not possible to affect large animals,except for a few species.For this the bird world is more represented arsenically

The island of Hispaniola is divided in two countries Dominican Republic and Haiti.2/3 of the island belongs to Dominican Republic and 1/3 to Haiti. Although both countries are on an island the culture, the landscape and social differences are huge.

The varied climate, geography, as well as plants and fauna found in the country should not only attract you to visit,but invite you to explore...

History

The island of Hispaniola was the first colony in the New World settled by Spain.

As such,it served as the Logistical base for the conquest of the most of the Western Hemisphere.Christopher Columbus first sighted the island in 1492 toward the end of the first voyage to "the Indies"

For two centuries Spanish ruled the island until western Hispaniola (present Haiti) came under French control in 1697. The east still remained under Spanish control. This created division that still exists today with Dominican Republic and Haiti sharing the island.

Finally, and after decades of unstable political situation on 27 February 1844 , the territory of santo Dominco recovered its sovereignty and declared its Independence as the Dominican Republic.
